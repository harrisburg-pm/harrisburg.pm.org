Who are the Harrisburg Perl Mongers?

The Harrisburg Perl Mongers are an informal group of geeks who get together to learn more about the Perl programming language. Our goal is to get together once a month to talk about Perl and other interesting things. Feel free to join us both on the mailing list and at the meetings. Organized get-togethers in addition to our once a month meeting are also welcomed; watch on the mailing list for these. You don't have to be a Perl hacker to fit in: if you are not and we can convey that perl is enough of an interest, then we have done our job.

Getting involved

You can go here to sign up for the mailing list.

You can also join us on IRC! #harrisburg.pm on irc.perl.org See you there!

About the Organizer

Jason May is the lead organizer and is happy to field any inquiries you may have. He is active on Twitter and Github.

Topics of Interest

There are a lot of cool new things going on in Perl that are worth talking about. Some of these things include:

  • Moose: A "post-modern" object system that was inspired by CLOS, SmallTalk, and more
  • PSGI/Plack: A specification and toolkit for gluing together Perl and the Web
  • Perl 6: completely different language from Perl 5. Though new, it has some incredible features
